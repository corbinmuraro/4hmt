4-H Million Trees Project

  • Give millions of 4-H youth around the world the opportunity to get personally involved in the climate crisis
  • Reduce atmospheric CO2 while having fun planting millions of trees around the world
  • Provide youth with leadership opportunities
  • Encourage youth to start service-learning projects in areas they are passionate about

Shortly after watching the documentary "An Inconvenient Truth," a girl from Pacifica, CA felt the need to help slow global climate change. So she worked with her 4-H group to form the Million Tree Project. Six years later, her goal was achieved when the team planted the 1 millionth tree at Huddart Park in Woodside, CA. If you like what you see here and want to pitch in too, you are invited to join the project!

Progress

The 4-H Million Trees Project currently consists of:

  • 250,000 members
  • From 651 Clubs/Units
  • Spread across 335 counties in 61 US states and other nations

Together we have:

  • Planted 30 Million trees
  • Plan to plant an additional 10 million trees
